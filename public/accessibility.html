Accessibility Statement

Last updated June 4, 2026

Peter Jones Experience Design LLC (“Hona,” “we,” “us,” or “our”) is committed to making the Hona website and the Hona iOS app accessible to everyone, including people with disabilities. This statement explains the standards we work toward, what we have done so far, what is still in progress, and how to reach us if something is not working for you.

If you cannot access something on hona.fitness or in the Hona app, please email peter@pjxdesigns.com. We will do our best to provide the information you need in an alternative format and to fix the underlying issue.

1. Our commitment

We believe that an athlete, coach, or anyone curious about the product should be able to learn about Hona, sign up for the beta, and use the app regardless of the assistive technology they rely on. Accessibility is part of how we design and build Hona, not an afterthought, and we treat it as ongoing work rather than a one-time checkbox.

2. Scope

This statement applies to:

  • The Hona marketing website at hona.fitness, including the home page, screen previews, beta signup, and legal pages.
  • The Hona iOS app and its Apple Watch companion, distributed through Apple’s TestFlight during beta and through the App Store at general release.

It does not apply to third-party services we integrate with (for example Apple Health, Strava, Oura, Whoop, Google Firebase, or the Anthropic API). Those services are governed by their own accessibility practices.

3. Standard we aim for

We work toward conformance with the Web Content Accessibility Guidelines (WCAG) 2.1 at Level AA, published by the World Wide Web Consortium (W3C). WCAG 2.1 AA is the standard most commonly referenced under the Americans with Disabilities Act (ADA) for digital products, and is the standard our website and app are designed and reviewed against.

For the iOS app, we additionally follow Apple’s accessibility guidelines for VoiceOver, Dynamic Type, Reduce Motion, Increase Contrast, and other system-level assistive features.

4. What we have done

Steps we have taken so far include:

  • Semantic HTML structure on the website, with meaningful headings, landmarks, and labelled controls.
  • Color and contrast tokens designed against the WCAG AA 4.5:1 text-contrast threshold (and 3:1 for large text).
  • Visible keyboard focus on all interactive elements on the website.
  • Descriptive alt text on meaningful images, and explicit aria-hidden on decorative ones (such as the hero phone video).
  • Native iOS controls in the Hona app, which inherit VoiceOver labels, Dynamic Type, and Reduce Motion support from the system by default.
  • Captions and labels on numerical readouts (readiness score, training load, macronutrients) so they can be read aloud by VoiceOver and other screen readers.

5. Known limitations

We want to be transparent about where we are still improving:

  • The hero animation on hona.fitness plays once on page load. It honors prefers-reduced-motion at the browser level for some browsers but not all; we are working on a fully respected motion setting.
  • Some in-app charts (training load, body composition trends) currently communicate primarily through visuals. We are working on text alternatives and screen-reader descriptions for the underlying data.
  • The app has not yet been audited by an independent third-party accessibility specialist. We plan to commission an audit before general App Store release.
  • Beta builds may contain regressions. If you find one, please tell us, see Section 7.

6. Assistive technology we test with

During development we routinely test with:

  • VoiceOver on iOS and iPadOS
  • Dynamic Type at large and accessibility-large sizes
  • Reduce Motion and Increase Contrast on iOS
  • Keyboard-only navigation on the website
  • VoiceOver on macOS and NVDA on Windows against the website

7. Feedback and how to report an issue

If you run into an accessibility barrier on hona.fitness or in the Hona app, or if you would like content in an alternative format, please contact:

Peter Jones
Peter Jones Experience Design LLC
Email: peter@pjxdesigns.com

Please include:

  • The page (URL) or app screen where you encountered the issue.
  • The assistive technology and operating system you were using (for example, “VoiceOver on iOS 18”).
  • A short description of what happened and what you expected.

We aim to respond within five business days. If the issue is blocking your ability to use a part of the product, we will prioritize it and provide an interim alternative (for example, sending requested information by email) while we work on a fix.

8. Formal complaints

If you have raised an accessibility concern with us and are not satisfied with the response, you may pursue a complaint through your local consumer-protection or disability-rights authority. In the United States, the U.S. Department of Justice maintains information on filing an ADA complaint at ada.gov/file-a-complaint.

9. Changes to this statement

We will update this statement as the website and app evolve and as we make further accessibility improvements. The “Last updated” date at the top of this page reflects the most recent revision.
